Jeff Masters - Tedx


In the video above by Jeff Masters (which is also the primary inspiration for this project), he gives an overview on the effects of climate change on hurricanes and droughts. In summary, as the temperature rises in the ocean, it provides more energy for hurricanes, making them much stronger and more devastating. Warmer climates increases the severity of droughts as well. Jeff Masters even goes on to predict the possibility of storms and the amount of damage they can cause (The most notable being Hurricane Sandy).
His website, where he discusses weather events that occur throughout the world is here

National Geographic - Pollution from Asia makes Pacific Storms Stronger

In the article above from National Geographic, it discusses the affect of pollution from Asia affecting storms in the Pacific. From the article, the aerosol particles coming in from Asia are making the storms stronger and are even altering the shape of the clouds. This can ultimately affect weather in North America.
